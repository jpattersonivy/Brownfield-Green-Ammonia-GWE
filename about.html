How Osmotic Energy Works

Osmotic energy, sometimes called blue energy, generates electricity from the natural difference in salt concentration between freshwater and seawater.

Step 1

Freshwater Meets Seawater

Freshwater flowing from rivers meets seawater in estuaries, creating a difference in salinity.

Step 2

Selective Membranes

Specialized membranes allow water or ions to move across them, producing pressure or electrical potential.

Step 3

Electricity Generation

The resulting energy drives turbines or electrochemical systems that produce renewable electricity without combustion.

Step 4

Environmental Monitoring

Sensors continuously monitor water quality, biodiversity, and energy production to ensure sustainable operation.
